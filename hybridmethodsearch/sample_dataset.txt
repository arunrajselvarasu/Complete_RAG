Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) combines retrieval and generation.
RAG enables language models to use external knowledge sources.
This improves answer accuracy and reduces hallucinations.
ChromaDB is a vector database used in AI applications.
It stores embeddings and supports semantic search.
Vector databases are commonly used in RAG systems.
Embeddings convert text into numerical vectors.
Similar meanings produce similar vectors.
Embeddings are the foundation of semantic search.
Dense retrieval uses embeddings to find relevant documents.
Sparse retrieval uses keyword matching techniques like BM25.
Hybrid search combines dense and sparse retrieval methods.
Max Marginal Relevance (MMR) reduces duplicate retrieval results.
It increases diversity among retrieved documents.
MMR helps provide broader context to language models.
Re-ranking improves retrieval quality.
A reranker reorders retrieved documents by relevance.
This often improves the final answer quality.
